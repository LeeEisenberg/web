Background


Droughts can cause many health problems. Some of the health effects directly related to droughts can be clearly observed and measured in the short term. However, the slow escalation or prolonged nature of droughts can also lead to indirect health effects over a long period of time that are not always easy to predict or monitor.


Health Implications of Droughts

  • Shortages and poor water quality can have a negative impact on air quality, sanitation, hygiene, and food and nutrition.
  • People may face more health problems due to lack of adequate drinking water.
  • A decline in the quality of drinking water can lead to a range of sanitation problems, while also potentially exacerbating the spread of disease.

    How Does Drought Affect our Lives?

    1. Economic Impacts

      The economic impact is mainly related to agriculture, timber industry, ship sales, power companies, water companies, and water-related businesses.

      Farmers could face crop losses and additional irrigation costs, ranchers could pay more for animal feed and water, and agriculture-related businesses could lose business.

      The timber industry has been affected by forest fires, and ship sales and aquaculture may not be able to sell goods.

      Hydroelectric companies may need to rely on other fuels, while power companies and water utilities may need to invest more to secure water supplies.

      Falling water levels could lead to difficulties in the water transport industry and people may have to pay higher food bills.
      Drought leads to 80% jump in vegetable prices

      In November, the cost of vegetables from producers surged by 38% on a monthly basis, marking an increase of over 80% compared to the same month in 2021 according to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics latest Producer Price Index.

      As a result, the impact of drought on the economy spans multiple sectors and levels.

    2. Social Impacts

      The multifaceted impacts of drought on society include anxiety over economic losses, health problems related to water quality and flow problems:

      1. Health problems from air quality
      2. Loss of life
      3. Increased fire threats to public safety
      4. Loss of income
      5. Migration flows and reduced recreational activities

      These demonstrate the profound effects of drought on all levels of society.
